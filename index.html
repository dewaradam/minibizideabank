Honest mechanics, same as your other models:
1 — LOW TOUCH IS EARNED, NOT FREE. "Low ongoing touch" almost always means high UPFRONT touch — the hyperfocus build is where the work lives. AI scales delivery, not the initial setup, positioning, or first customers.
2 — "IMMEDIATE INCOME" IS THE EXCEPTION. A few (printables, POD, lead-gen) can sell day one. Most need an audience, a partner, or proof first. Don't confuse "fast to launch" with "fast to revenue."
3 — THE HANDOFF VISION IS REAL BUT NEEDS STRUCTURE. Gifting a cash-flowing micro-business to your kids or a non-profit is doable, but earned-income effects on benefits, labor classification, and who owns the IP all need the same specialist counsel flagged in your care-facility work.
4 — REGULATED CORNERS. Anything with food (eggs, honey, microgreens, treats), kids' care, health/diet claims, or financial advice carries licensing or liability you must check locally — Michigan cottage-food and home-business rules included.
5 — AI-PRODUCT IP & DISCLOSURE. Selling AI-generated stories, art, voices, or text raises ownership, likeness, and disclosure questions that are still shifting. Verify current rules before you sell at volume — don't trust a one-year-old read of the law.
